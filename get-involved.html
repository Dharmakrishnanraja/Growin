Get Involved

Two ways in: your time, or your support.

GROWIN runs on people who show up regularly more than on any single large gift. If you've got a few hours a month, we'd love to meet you. If you'd rather support a program directly, we'll show you exactly where it goes.

Volunteer with us

Roles we're building our founding circle around

No experience required for most roles — just consistency. Pick what fits your week.

Program Volunteer

Help run skill batches, health camps and community sessions on the ground — a couple of hours, a few times a month.

Skill Trainer

Teach what you know — tailoring, computer basics, spoken English, a trade — to one of our Academy batches.

Digital & Content Volunteer

Photos, social posts, simple design, updates to this website — remote-friendly, work around your own schedule.

Event Support Crew

Registration desks, logistics, setup and crowd support on event days — high energy, low commitment.

"Programs" covers training materials, trainer honorariums, camp supplies and saplings. "Org & Capacity Lab" covers workshop materials and travel for partner-group sessions. "Admin" covers the basics — registration, communication and record-keeping.

For organisations & businesses

CSR partnerships with GROWIN

Under Section 135 of the Companies Act, eligible companies must direct CSR spending toward activities listed in Schedule VII — and every GROWIN program area fits squarely within it: education, healthcare, women's empowerment, rural development, and environmental sustainability.

As a newly registered trust, we're upfront that our 80G approval is pending — but we're already a good fit for in-kind support (sewing machines, books, health-camp supplies, saplings), employee volunteering days, or a pilot-funded program in a specific village.

  • Sponsor a full Skill Academy batch or a Community Learning Centre
  • Organise an employee volunteering day — health camp, plantation drive
  • In-kind support: machines, books, devices, medical supplies, saplings
  • Co-design a pilot program for a specific village or worker community
