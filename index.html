About GROWIN

Built in Rajapalayam — for families, and for the groups who serve them.

Most welfare trusts stop at giving out help. GROWIN was set up to do that and something else: strengthen the small self-help groups, temple committees, school associations and youth clubs that already exist in every village — so that the support they offer their own communities keeps getting stronger, with or without us.

We're based at Dharmapuram, Rajapalayam — Tamil Nadu's "cotton city" — and our first programs focus on skill-building, education support, women's collectives, health outreach, environment and, for partner organisations, plain-language training in governance, record-keeping and digital tools.

What we do

Six programs, one idea: growth that's earned, not given.

Every program pairs a real, local need with a skill, a habit or a system that stays useful long after we've moved on to the next village.

01 / Skill & Livelihood

Skill & Livelihood Academy

Hands-on batches in tailoring, mobile & electronics repair and basic computer use — so youth and women in Rajapalayam can earn with dignity, close to home.

Learn more
02 / Education

Community Learning Centres

After-school support, spoken English and digital basics for children — because a fair start shouldn't depend on what a family can afford in tuition.

Learn more
03 / Women's Collective

Women's Collective (SHG Network)

We help women form and run self-help groups: savings discipline, micro-enterprise ideas, and a support network that outlasts any one program.

Learn more
04 / Health

Health & Wellness Outreach

Regular health camps, awareness sessions and referral support, run with local doctors and healthcare volunteers who know the area.

Learn more
05 / Environment

Green Rajapalayam

Tree planting, waste segregation awareness and clean-water initiatives — small actions, repeated often, across the taluk.

Learn more
06 / Org Capacity

Org & Capacity Lab

Free, plain-language workshops for SHGs, temple committees and youth clubs on registration, bookkeeping, fundraising and basic digital tools.

Learn more

How we work

One process, repeated honestly in every village.

Listen

We start with home visits and panchayat meetings — understanding what a community actually needs, not what's easiest for us to deliver.

Design

Together with local volunteers and partner groups, we shape a program to fit the place — not a template copied from elsewhere.

Deliver

Training batches, camps and support sessions run with local trainers wherever possible, building local capacity as we go.

Track & Share

We record attendance, outcomes and feedback — and publish what we learn, good or not-so-good, on our transparency page.
